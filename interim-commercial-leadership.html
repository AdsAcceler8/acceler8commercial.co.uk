The reality

"The businesses that fall behind rarely lack a strategy. They lack the senior commercial resource to execute it — while the permanent hire search runs."

Commercial leadership gaps are expensive. Not just because of what doesn't get done — deals that drift, revenue targets that slip, pricing discipline that softens — but because the absence of a senior commercial voice at the critical moment compounds over time.

A permanent hire for a commercial director or Chief Commercial Officer typically takes three to six months to recruit, and several more to reach full effectiveness. Businesses at a growth inflection point, navigating a commercial transformation, or covering an unexpected departure rarely have that window.

An interim commercial director steps in immediately — with the seniority, sector knowledge, and commercial judgment to hold the function together and drive it forward. A fractional CCO provides the same quality of leadership on an ongoing, part-time basis: senior commercial thinking at a fraction of the full-time cost.

What interim commercial leadership delivers

The full weight of a CCO,
without the full-time
overhead.

Interim commercial leadership is not a holding pattern. The most valuable engagements move things forward — strategy, revenue, team capability — from day one.

The scope of an interim commercial director engagement varies with what the business needs most. In some cases that is immediate revenue leadership — taking ownership of pipeline, sales process, and commercial strategy while the permanent hire is found. In others, it is transformation: redesigning the commercial function, repositioning pricing, or preparing the business commercially for a fundraise or acquisition.

What doesn't change is the standard. Senior commercial leadership means owning outcomes — not producing slide decks for someone else to implement.

What the engagement typically covers

Commercial strategy and revenue model — defining or resetting the commercial strategy, revenue model, and go-to-market approach for the business's current stage and objectives.

Pipeline and deal leadership — direct involvement in major deals: structuring terms, managing negotiations, and ensuring commercial momentum is maintained while permanent leadership is established.

Pricing and margin discipline — establishing or restoring pricing rigour: frameworks, approval processes, and the commercial training that prevents margin erosion at the point of sale.

Commercial team leadership — leading, coaching and structuring the commercial team — sales, commercial operations, bid management — to raise capability and performance during the transition.

Board-level commercial reporting — providing the board or CEO with clear, credible commercial reporting: pipeline health, revenue forecasting, deal risk, and the commercial metrics that inform investment decisions.

Commercial infrastructure — building or strengthening the foundations: commercial playbooks, governance frameworks, contract templates, and the processes that let the commercial function scale without breaking.

Interim or fractional

Two models,
one standard of
commercial leadership.

The right engagement model depends on what the business needs and how quickly. Both provide senior, independent commercial leadership — without the cost and lead time of a permanent hire.

Interim

Full-time or near-full-time commercial leadership for a defined period — typically three to twelve months. The right model when a business needs comprehensive commercial cover: covering a departure, managing a transformation, or holding the function together during a significant commercial event.

Deployable within days. Full focus on your business for the duration of the engagement.

From days notice
Fractional CCO

Senior commercial leadership on an ongoing, part-time basis — typically one to three days per week. The right model when a business needs consistent commercial oversight and strategic input without the cost of a full-time executive hire.

Embedded in the business over time. Commercial leadership that builds capability, not just output.
